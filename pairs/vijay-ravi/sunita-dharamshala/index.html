Donor first, क्योंकि उनका self-interest तुम्हारे साथ है: उनको पैसा वापस चाहिए, और उनको Naddi का पानी project नए shape में पूरा करवाना है, क्योंकि वो ख़ुद उस impact report पे dependent हैं अपने अगले funding round के लिए.

Ravi: Sunita बहन, मैं Bhai के बाद कुछ practical paper-वाली बातें जोड़ता हूँ. पहली — FCRA Annual Return mechanism एक useful safety net है, पर पहली line of defence नहीं. हर FCRA-registered NGO को form FC-4 file करना होता है हर साल, और auditor signature के साथ — यह public record है, और donor की hotline activate होने के बाद इसमें discrepancy पकड़ी जा सकती है, यह तुम्हारी claim को एक independent crystallised paper trail देती है. दूसरी — IndiaWaterPortal.org और Centre for Policy Research की accountability portals NGO accountability पे काम करती हैं — एक बार donor-side action शुरू हो जाए, इन में से किसी एक के साथ confidential consultation रखी जा सकती है, यह तुम्हें एक Indian advocacy backup देता है अगर donor process stall हो. तीसरी — board chair अक्सर executive director से अलग व्यक्ति होता है, और कई बार genuinely independent — पर यह decide तुम Sunday तक नहीं कर सकती, करो जब donor की reply आए, ताकि तुम्हें पता हो chair किस tone में react करेगा. चौथी — Tata Trusts fellowship वालों से अपनी situation discreetly share कर सकती हो (alumni network), उन्होंने ही तुम्हें इस field में डाला है, उनकी पास social-sector में बहुत network है, और एक नई position वो ख़ुद recommend कर सकते हैं. आख़िरी — और सबसे ज़रूरी — अपने gaon के बारे में एक बात याद रखना, बहन. Project का देर से पूरा होना recoverable है — पाइप थोड़ी देर से लगे, यह तीन-छह महीने की देरी है, gaon वहीं रहेगा, पानी आख़िर पहुँचेगा. Project का एक झूठ पे खड़ा होना — यह recoverable नहीं है. एक झूठ पे खड़ा project tomorrow पाइप leak करता है, परसों maintenance नहीं होती, अगले साल कोई और गाँव में डाल देता है यह project अपने नाम पे और तुम्हारे gaon में सिर्फ़ अधूरा concrete बच जाता है. एक true project एक छोटे delay के बाद full ज़िंदगी जीता है. यह trade तुम्हारे gaon के लिए भी सही है, सिर्फ़ तुम्हारे conscience के लिए नहीं. Bhai और मैं — हम तुम्हारे साथ हैं इस letter में, और जिस दिन तुम लिखोगी "donor ने reply भेजी," हम वहाँ भी होंगे.

Summary

Sunita, 29, is a field officer at a mid-size NGO in Dharamshala, Himachal Pradesh, four years into her first job. She is from Naddi village above McLeodganj — a daily-wage labourer's daughter, the eldest of four siblings, the first salaried person from her village. A Tata Trusts fellowship at Azim Premji University put her on this track, and her director, Mr. K, wrote the recommendation that got her this job. The NGO's current project is a ₹38 lakh European-donor-funded gravity-fed water supply for three hamlets in Naddi — her own village, the basti just below her father's house. She maintains the muster rolls. Last month, working late, she accidentally saw a salary ledger she did not have access to. Twenty-two "labourers" were billed who do not exist on her actual register; the names are not local; two Aadhaars she checked are dummy. Roughly ₹4-5 lakh has been skimmed in the current phase. The donor audit is six weeks away. A new batch of muster rolls sits on her desk this week, awaiting her signature. If she signs, her handwriting joins the lie. If she escalates: her job goes, the project may freeze halfway, the village's water supply stalls, and her name in Dharamshala's small NGO circuit becomes "the one who burned her boss." Her father, two younger sisters in school, and a brother in Class 12 sit behind her economically. Vijay names the trap clearly: the director's gamble is that her gratitude — fellowship, salary raises, his wife tutoring her sister — has bought her silence. He calls that gamble false because she did not come from Naddi to sign fake muster rolls; she has to refuse it without making it personal. Ravi pushes back on Vijay's instinct that "all the official routes are paper, not ground" — European donor fraud-reporting hotlines are serious, confidential, and structurally aligned with Sunita's interests because the donor wants its money back and its impact report intact. They land on a sequenced four-step plan for this very week. One: stop signing the new muster roll using a "process change" cover — claim a recent compliance webinar suggested co-signature requirements, ask a senior colleague to countersign — buying fifteen to twenty days without alerting the director. Two: quietly assemble a folder on a personal laptop — six months of muster rolls, project budget vs. ground reality, donor contract clauses, the screenshots, ghost-labourer names, monthly visit reports, EXIF-tagged worksite photographs — and email a password-protected ZIP to her own personal Gmail every Friday so a server-timestamped chain of evidence exists outside office systems. Three: find the donor's whistleblower or "Integrity Line" or "Speak Up" channel on their website and submit the full dossier under her own name (not anonymously — anonymity weakens credibility and follow-up; her insider status as a Naddi resident and four-year field officer is her strongest credential). Four: keep her own integrity record locked — every verbal instruction converted into a "just to confirm what we discussed" email, every visit report and signed document discreetly bcc'd to her personal Gmail, so that when the audit comes the director cannot frame her as a co-author. Indian-side routes — board chair, FCRA Annual Return mechanism, NGO Darpan complaint, IndiaWaterPortal accountability portals — sit second in the queue, to be activated only if the donor process stalls, because executive directors often share friendships with board chairs and FCRA processes are slow. Ravi adds the structural reassurances: FCRA form FC-4 leaves a public paper trail; the Tata Trusts alumni network may be a discreet job-side safety net; and the deepest line is for the village. A water project delayed by three to six months is recoverable — pipes still get laid, water still arrives. A water project built on a lie is not recoverable; it leaks tomorrow, fails maintenance the year after, and leaves the village with half-laid concrete. The true project, even after a delay, lives a full life. Both brothers tell her plainly that the trade is right not just for her conscience but for Naddi itself, and that they will still be there when she writes back to say the donor has replied.
