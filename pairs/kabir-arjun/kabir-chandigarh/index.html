Arjun: Kabir from Chandigarh — हाँ, अजीब लग रहा है name से address करना, but bear with me. आप पूछते हो — "बेकार दोस्त हूँ या honest?" Summary

Kabir, 36, IT professional in Chandigarh, has been writing the best man speech for his oldest friend Aman's wedding in Goa next month — fifteen years of friendship through DAV, Thapar, and Bangalore. Every draft of the speech ends up being about Kabir himself: a relationship he walked away from in 2018, the choice to leave Bangalore and come back to Chandigarh, the bigger life he didn't lead. He has three weeks left and no working speech. He asks if this makes him a bad friend or an honest one. The pair lean into the awkward fact that the asker shares Kabir's name — and Arjun takes the lead deliberately, because he is the one with this exact disease. The diagnosis: Kabir is not a bad friend; bad friends don't agonise for six weeks. He is using Aman's wedding as a mirror, and seeing the man he didn't become. The mirror is real and useful — but the wedding mic is not where it belongs. Separate the two chapters. The speech is about Aman: three concrete things only a best friend would know. The mirror is for after — a long night, drinks, three hours of honesty Aman deserves and Kabir owes himself. Then a trip to Bangalore alone, to ask the real question. Aman's day is Aman's. Kabir's reckoning gets its own.
