Summary

Tanveer Ahmed, 35, a senior IT lead in Bengaluru, changed companies two years ago. The standard PF transfer request — a routine online process — has been "Pending with Source Office" since day one. He has filed eleven grievances on the EPFO portal. All eleven were auto-closed within 17 days as "resolved." Nothing was resolved. ₹4.6 lakh is stuck. He has a spreadsheet. Old company's HR doesn't reply. New company's HR can't help. He is an engineer who builds systems, and a system is beating him. Gabbar diagnoses the mistake immediately: he has been knocking on the same dustbin door eleven times. The online grievance portal is a closed loop with no human in it; "resolved" means a bot timed out, not a person acted. Three fronts, opened simultaneously, this week. One: physical visit to EPFO's Bengaluru Regional Office in Koramangala — printed file, named officer, repeat visits if necessary. Two: CPGRAMS escalation (separate portal, Ministry of Labour & Employment, real response within 30 days). Three: a public Twitter thread tagging the Labour Minister, PMO, EPFO, and a Bengaluru MP — pixelated screenshots of the Excel — because every Bengaluru IT worker shares this fear and it will travel. Stop opening the portal every Monday — it is grinding him down for no reason. Set a single Friday 4pm calendar slot. Samba adds the final option: an RTI under the EPF Act asking for the assigned officer, last action date, and the reason each grievance was closed without resolution. RTI surfaces a name. Once there is a name, there is accountability. The portal is the trap. The way out is everywhere except the portal.
