Ludhiana के अपने MLA या nearby MLA के office में जा — हर MLA का एक "RTI cell" या "Education cell" volunteer होता है — उसको एक copy दे, और एक छोटी सी request — "MLA साहब को बताइए, एक 19 साल के OBC student की college admission रुकी हुई है format-pretext पर." साथ-साथ Punjabi Tribune, Ajit, Jagbani, Hindustan Times Ludhiana edition के education desk पे एक tip — एक छोटी सी story: "Ludhiana college clerk asks ₹500 from loom worker's son, then rejects valid OBC certificate." जिस सुबह यह छपती है, उसी दोपहर तक college के Principal के table पे चार phone आते हैं — DC office से, MLA से, UGC regional office से, और एक party leader से. Ramesh Kumar उसी हफ़्ते में 'reconsidered' कर के certificate accept कर लेता है — यह जादू नहीं है, यह सरकारी डर है. सरकार जनता से नहीं डरती, सरकार छपने से डरती है.

Insurance — Tehsildar Office. समानांतर में Tehsildar Office Ludhiana में Form D-7 आज ही जमा कर — सब documents के साथ, urgent application stamp लगवा. Tehsildar खुद OBC/SC/ST community का हो तो 7-10 दिन में निकाल देता है. यह parallel track है, सिर्फ़ insurance.

Plan B — High Court writ. 22 अगस्त को admission lock है, अगर 19-20 तक भी जवाब नहीं आ रहा, तो Punjab and Haryana High Court में एक writ petition party-in-person — Punjab State Legal Services Authority Chandigarh मुफ़्त वकील देती है. "Petitioner, OBC, valid certificate, JEE allotment, college rejection without notification" — यह writ HC 48 घंटे में admit करता है और एक interim direction निकाल सकता है college को कि admission allow करो जब तक मामला नहीं सुना. यह आख़िरी रास्ता है, पर असली है, और मुफ़्त है.

और अब एक बात — पिताजी से और माँ से. आज रात उनको बैठा, चाय बना ख़ुद से, और बोल — "पिताजी, मैं चुप नहीं बैठा हूँ. मैं पाँच जगह file डाल रहा हूँ — College, RTI, UGC, NCBC, MLA-newspaper. एक clerk की हिम्मत नहीं है हम पाँच के सामने. आपका PF, माँ का मंगलसूत्र — एक hostage नहीं हुआ है, यह सब वापस आएगा. मुझे 14 दिन चाहिए, और मैं हर रात आपको update दूँगा." पिताजी loom-worker हैं — वो जानते हैं system कैसे चलता है, उन्होंने 30 साल देखा है — पर वो यह नहीं जानते कि अब system पे एक बच्चा भी पाँच दरवाज़ों से चढ़ सकता है. यह तू उनको बताएगा, और वो रात में दो बार पानी पीने नहीं उठेंगे.

Samba: Gurmeet भाई, Sardar के बाद एक बात मेरी तरफ़ से. आपने एक बहुत बड़ी बात लिखी है — "मैं अभी न student हूँ, न मज़दूर हूँ, मेरी पहचान ही खो गई है." यह line मेरे दिल में बैठ गई. पर सच यह है — आप student हैं, सरकारी कागज़ पे एक allotment letter आपके नाम पे है, fee receipt आपके नाम पे है, JEE rank आपके नाम पे है. एक clerk उस पहचान को छीन नहीं सकता — वो सिर्फ़ रोक सकता है, और रोकना अलग बात है छीनने से. आप पाँच दरवाज़े एक हफ़्ते में खोलिए — पहले दरवाज़े का receipt लेकर अगले दरवाज़े जाइए, यह एक chain reaction है. एक छोटा सा daily routine — हर शाम अपनी छोटी notebook में लिखो: कौन सा दरवाज़ा खुला, किस officer ने क्या बोला, कब का receipt है. यह notebook आपके future case की रीढ़ है, और आपके पिताजी को रोज़ रात दिखाने का सबूत है कि बेटा लड़ रहा है, हार नहीं मान रहा. एक आख़िरी बात — Sardar Bhagat Singh भी 19 साल के थे जब उन्होंने पहली पर्चा छपवाया था. आप भी 19 के हैं. एक clerk के mood पे आपका future नहीं rests करता — आपके पाँच दरवाज़ों पे rests करता है, और वो दरवाज़े आप ख़ुद खोलोगे. हम — Sardar और मैं — इस चिट्ठी में आपके साथ खड़े हैं. लड़ो भाई, और जीतोगे.

Summary

Gurmeet Singh, 19, lives in Basti Jodhewal, Ludhiana — his father is a powerloom worker earning thirteen and a half thousand a month, his mother stitches at home, his fourteen-year-old brother is in school. He is the first college-goer in his family. He scored 84 percent in Class 12 and 92.6 percentile in JEE Main, and through OBC quota in the third counselling round he got a Mechanical Engineering seat at a Punjab government engineering college on the Patiala campus. First-year fees were thirty-eight thousand rupees. His father took a forty-thousand-rupee loan against his Provident Fund. His mother pawned her mangalsutra. He resigned from his nine-thousand-rupee helper job at Hero Cycles on 3 August, with college starting 25 August. On 8 August he went for physical document verification carrying a 2022-issued OBC certificate from Tehsildar Office Ludhiana — the same certificate JEE Counselling had already accepted for his allotment letter and fee receipt. The Section C admission cell clerk, Ramesh Kumar, told him the format was outdated, that he must reapply on Form D-7 with fresh photographs, Aadhaar, his father's income certificate and ration card, and that the new certificate would take sixty days. Admission lock is 22 August — fourteen days. As Gurmeet stood stunned in the corridor, a typist in a white kurta with a stamp-vendor licence number 1247, calling himself S. Mehta, offered to "expedite" the certificate for five hundred rupees in seven days. Gurmeet paid; three days later the stall was empty and the chai-wallah said this typist rotated through that corridor every two weeks. Crucially, his sister-in-law's nephew, a law student at Punjabi University, has confirmed that no Punjab Government notification exists invalidating pre-2024 OBC certificates — Ramesh Kumar's "outdated format" rule is invented, either to extract money or to suppress OBC seats. Gabbar reads the letter and names it for what it is: a clerk's mood masquerading as a rule, leaning on the family of a loom worker because he has counted on them being too tired and too unconnected to fight back. The fix is five doors opened in parallel within fourteen days, because no single clerk's mood can hold five doors at once. One: a written grievance to the College Principal today, with a stamped receipt, citing the JEE counselling acceptance and demanding the written rule under which the certificate was rejected. Two: a ten-rupee RTI to the State Public Information Officer, Department of Higher Education Punjab, with three precise questions about any notification invalidating pre-2024 OBC certificates, why JEE counselling accepted what the college rejected, and how many OBC seats the college has rejected on format ground over three years — invoking Section 7(1) urgent provision since admission is at stake within fourteen days. Three: an online complaint to the UGC SC/ST/OBC Cell at ugc.gov.in naming Ramesh Kumar and counter, with the law student's signed note attached — UGC can issue a directive to the Principal that overrides a counter clerk's whim within days. Four: a constitutional complaint to the National Commission for Backward Classes at ncbc.nic.in, which after the 102nd Amendment can compel a written college response within a week because non-compliance becomes a constitutional matter. Five: walk into the local MLA's office, drop a copy with the RTI cell volunteer, and walk into Punjabi Tribune, Ajit, Jagbani and Hindustan Times Ludhiana edition's education desks with a tip — "Ludhiana college clerk takes ₹500 from loom worker's son, then rejects valid OBC certificate." The morning that prints, four phones land on the Principal's desk. Separately, a complaint to the Treasury Officer Ludhiana citing receipt and licence number 1247 to cancel S. Mehta's stamp vendor licence, cleaning that corridor for the next student. As insurance, Form D-7 in parallel at Tehsildar Office Ludhiana — but the real fight is not at the Tehsildar's, it is at the college clerk's. As Plan B, if 22 August nears without resolution, a writ petition party-in-person at Punjab and Haryana High Court with free counsel from Punjab State Legal Services Authority — these matters get admitted within forty-eight hours and interim directions for temporary admission are routine. Triplicate every document tonight; have the law student write a one-page certified note that no Punjab notification invalidates the pre-2024 format; keep a daily notebook of every officer, every receipt, every quote — both as evidence and as nightly proof to your father that you are fighting, not collapsing. The PF and the mangalsutra are not lost; they are temporarily hostage to one clerk's invented rule, and that rule does not survive five doors. He is not without identity — there is a JEE allotment letter and a fee receipt with his name on government servers, and one counter clerk cannot erase that, only delay it.
