Familia. यानी F-A-M-I-L-I-A. वो बोला "Sir, यह modern है, सब societies अब ऐसे नाम रखती हैं, और इसमें warmth है." मैंने उठ के objection रखा — "Family मतलब हम सब blood relations हैं? यह तो हम नहीं हैं, यह एक administrative group है." पर बहुमत ने vote कर दिया — 56 in favour, 31 against, 9 abstain. नाम change हो गया. अब WhatsApp group का नाम है "Shanti Nagar Familia 🏡✨" — emoji तक डाल दिए हैं, सर.

मेरे लिए यह छोटा issue नहीं है. एक — यह undignified है. हम 96 households हैं, हमारे beech में जातिगत, धार्मिक, भाषाई diversity है — Patel, Shah, Iyer, Kulkarni, Ansari, Sikh, Christian — हम "familia" नहीं हैं, हम civil neighbours हैं. दूसरा — यह linguistic colonialism है. Spanish/Italian word को हिंदी/गुजराती society पर थोपना मूर्खता है. तीसरा — emoji. यह एक 96-household की administrative communication channel है, यह कोई teenager का group chat नहीं है. चौथा — bank, society documents, audit reports में जब हम group screenshot लगाते हैं तो "Shanti Nagar Familia 🏡✨" लिखा होगा. यह officially embarrassing है. पाँचवाँ — हमारे elderly residents — मेरी पत्नी की माँ 78 की हैं, वो "familia" शब्द नहीं समझतीं. छठा — यह precedent set करता है. कल कोई "Shanti Nagar Squad" propose करेगा, फिर "Shanti Nagar Vibes." सातवाँ — यह decision एक AGM में रखा गया जहाँ language and naming पे पहले से कोई agenda नहीं था; यह proper procedure नहीं है.

मैंने सात-point objection एक A4 page पे type किया है, society के Secretary को email किया, और AGM minutes में formal addition भी request किया. Reply आया — "Patel sir, बहुमत हो गया है, आगे बढ़िए please." मेरी पत्नी बोलती है "तू पागल है क्या, छोड़ ना." मेरा बेटा (35, Bengaluru में IT) हँस के बोला "Papa, this is exactly the kind of thing we make memes about." मेरे committee colleagues मुझे avoid करने लगे हैं. पर मुझे लगता है मैं right हूँ. यह एक dignity का सवाल है. क्या मैं overreact कर रहा हूँ? और अगर नहीं, तो मेरा अगला कदम क्या है? मैं हार नहीं मानूँगा.

Summary

Chandrakant Patel, 55, is a retired Senior Manager from Bank of Baroda Operations and currently the Treasurer of his twelve-building, ninety-six-flat housing society in Bopal, Ahmedabad. He himself proposed the WhatsApp group name "Shanti Nagar Residents" at the 2017 AGM and the society used it without complaint for eight years. Last month a new resident's twenty-four-year-old son proposed renaming the group "Shanti Nagar Familia 🏡✨." The society voted 56 in favour, 31 against, 9 abstain — and the name changed. Chandrakant has typed a seven-point objection on an A4 page covering everything from undignified administrative communication, to linguistic colonialism, to AGM procedure, to embarrassment in audit screenshots, to elderly residents not understanding the word. The Secretary replied: majority has spoken, please move on. His wife told him he was being silly. His thirty-five-year-old IT son in Bengaluru called it "exactly the kind of thing we make memes about." His committee colleagues are quietly avoiding him. He wants to know whether he is overreacting and what his next step is. Teja and Gogo treat the matter with full mock-seriousness — debating whether the renaming is cultural infiltration, whether the nine abstentions are a conspiracy, whether the son's "meme material" remark is domestic-level betrayal — before landing the actual diagnosis through Gogo: Chandrakant is five percent right and ninety-five percent overreacting, because this is not a WhatsApp group problem, it is a retirement problem. A man who spent thirty-plus years running bank operations, whose mind is built to read rules, find gaps and write precedents, has been left with no large project for the past six months — and the same mind has fallen onto a small object and crushed it. The plan has two practical pieces and one personal trick. First, an honourable withdrawal in twelve words: "मेरी आपत्ति रिकॉर्ड पर है, बहुमत का सम्मान करता हूँ. आगे बढ़ते हैं." This protects all his future political capital. Second, immediately propose to the committee a project worthy of his actual capacity — a Society Bye-Laws review, a five-year maintenance cost audit, a solar feasibility study, or a senior citizens' charter — any one of which will redirect the same seven-point energy onto a problem the same energy can actually solve. The personal trick: WhatsApp lets each user set a custom chat name visible only on their own phone, so he can keep seeing "Shanti Nagar Residents" forever, a silent private victory nobody else needs to know about. Underneath it all, an honest conversation with his wife and son: I'm not as busy as I look; I miss having a real project; I'll find one. That confession will land with both of them like nothing else in years.
