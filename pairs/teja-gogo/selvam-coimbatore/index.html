Summary

Selvam from Coimbatore is being slowly destroyed by a decorative windmill in his neighbour's garden that squeaks at 3am and again at 4am, every night, for the past six months. ₹450 noise-cancelling earplugs have failed. The neighbour, however, is a genuinely good man who sends sweets at Diwali. Teja and Gogo treat the situation like a matter of national security, get sidetracked debating the moral weight of kaju katli vs besan ladoo, and finally land on the only sensible plan: don't ask, don't complain, don't escalate. Buy a ₹250 can of WD-40, walk over on Sunday morning, compliment the windmill, and oil it together. Win the war without your enemy ever knowing one was fought.
