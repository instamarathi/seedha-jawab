Summary

Gopal, 38, runs Gopal Tours and Travels in Jaipur — six buses, the Jaipur–Pushkar–Ajmer–Khatu Shyam pilgrim circuit, foreign-tourist Rajasthan packages, school trips. Eight years in business, 4.6 average across 312 Google reviews. Nineteen days ago, a stranger called RJ_traveller_2026 left a one-star review with two lines — "Bus smelled. Driver rude." — and the average dropped to 4.4. The bus had been professionally cleaned the day before, the driver has a five-year clean record, and Gopal has drafted six replies in his Notes app, including one that began "tu BJP wala hai kya," and deleted all six. Teja and Gogo treat the matter as a possible coordinated review attack by either Pushkar Tours or Mogambo's men, then arrive at the only sensible plan: post one short, dignified, factual reply tonight, don't chase Google removal, run a one-week campaign asking the last fifty satisfied customers for honest reviews to bury the bad one, and stop checking the listing more than once a week on Friday afternoon. The review is not the problem; the checking is.
