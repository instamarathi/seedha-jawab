Raju: Lakshmi, मेरी बात Rancho की हर बात के बाद है — और यह बात Rancho नहीं बोल सकता क्योंकि उसके पापा हमेशा rich थे और उसको कभी किसी से approval नहीं चाहिए थी. मेरे पापा भी ऐसे ही बोलते थे — "बेटा, सरकारी ले ले, सरकारी." मेरे एक चाचा railway में clerk थे, हर बार example. मुझे यह डर अंदर से पता है — एक middle-class घर में बेटी होना, घर का primary earner होना, parents का बूढ़ा होना — यह सब real भार है, और इसको Rancho कभी पूरी तरह feel नहीं कर सकता क्योंकि उसने अनुभव नहीं किया. मैंने किया है. मेरे एक dost ने सरकारी ली थी इसी डर से — आज 35 का है, हर हफ़्ते LinkedIn देखता है उन friends का जिन्होंने MBA लिया था. वो दर्द लगातार है. एक चीज़ मेरे पापा ने मुझे बहुत बाद में बोला, मेरे काम चलने के बाद — "बेटा, उस वक़्त मैं डरा हुआ था, अब समझ आता है." पापा भी इंसान हैं, वो भी ग़लत हो सकते हैं डर में, और वो भी बाद में आ कर "मैं ग़लत था" बोल सकते हैं. यह नहीं चाहिए कि वो ग़लत होकर बोलें — यह चाहिए कि तू अपनी सच के साथ खड़ी रहे, prepared, calm, और काग़ज़ से. एक last बात — हर महीने अपने आप से एक सवाल पूछ, MBA में रहते हुए: "क्या मुझे आज भी पसंद है?" अगर हाँ, चलती जा. अगर एक दिन ना हो, रुक के सोच. लेकिन जो डर तेरे पापा का है, उसको अपना मत बना. वो डर उनकी generation का है — वो उन्होंने अपनी ज़िंदगी में जिया, अब उनकी बारी है तेरी ज़िंदगी देखने की. और तू बेटी होने के नाते selfish नहीं हो रही — तू एक complete इंसान बन रही है, और complete इंसान ही पुरे घर को देख सकता है, थका हुआ इंसान नहीं.

Summary

Lakshmi, 26, is a software developer at TCS Chennai for the last three and a half years, ECE engineer from PSG Tech Coimbatore, and a 99.42 percentile CAT scorer who has accepted IIM Indore for May 2026 — total fees ₹23 lakh, of which ₹18 lakh will be an SBI education loan at 8.5% over ten years, EMI starting six months after MBA. Last Wednesday, an unrelated joining letter arrived: an IBPS Specialist Officer (IT) result from a backup exam she took three years ago has now reached her — IDBI Bank, Officer Scale-1, Mumbai posting, ₹8.4 LPA, permanent, gazetted-equivalent, pension. Joining last date January 31, 2026. She is the elder daughter and household primary earner — her father retired from Tamil Nadu Electricity Board with a ₹38,000 pension, mother is a retired teacher, younger brother Karthik is in B.Tech second year with relatives currently helping his fees. She has been sending ₹40,000 a month home from her TCS salary. Father has been repeating "beti, IDBI le le, security hai" for a week; mother sits with wet eyes; father said "if you go for MBA and something goes wrong, I am 65, what will we do?" Lakshmi cannot sleep. She wonders if she is selfish; if security is worth thirty years; how to talk to a father for whom "permanent" is a religion. Rancho refuses to issue a verdict — both fears in this story are real, neither parent nor daughter is wrong. The work is to convert two competing fears into one shared plan over the next six weeks. First, build a real five-year financial spreadsheet, both options, on a single A4 — IDBI year-by-year take-home and growth; IIM with a growing loan during years 1-2, conservative ₹18 LPA placement assumption (IIM Indore median is ₹24 LPA, last batch), EMI of ₹23-25,000 monthly from year 3, year-5 realistic positioning at ₹35-50 LPA. Even a worst-case ₹15 LPA outcome significantly outperforms IDBI by year 3 — that is the honest comparison her father can absorb because he is a TNEB man who has read meters and accounts for thirty-six years and trusts numbers more than emotion. Second, build a real twenty-four-month household buffer — ₹40,000 a month for two years means about ₹9.6 lakh — using four levers in parallel: ask TCS for a "Sponsored Higher Studies" sabbatical or unpaid leave; add a ₹3-4 lakh living-expenses component to the SBI education loan (formally legitimate); make Karthik responsible for his own ₹8-12,000/month from third-year part-time work or paid internships in Coimbatore; keep ₹1 lakh of TCS gratuity in the household account as an emergency cushion. Third, sit father down on a Sunday over morning chai with that one A4 paper and open with "Papa, mujhe aapki madad chahiye decide karne mein" — not with "I have decided." Show numbers, then ask one specific question — "Papa, if I take IDBI and five years from now you feel my life has been made smaller by it, how will you feel about yourself?" That sentence stops him because his real fear is not the loan, it is that he cannot help her at sixty-five if a risk goes wrong. Address that fear directly with a written commitment: she goes to MBA, the household monthly continues uninterrupted from buffer, and after placement, EMI plus household runs in parallel. The conversation is not about winning his approval — it is about converting his fear into a shared plan he can hold onto. And on the inner question — Lakshmi must remove the word "selfish" from her own vocabulary; that word lands faster on middle-class daughters than on sons by training, not by truth. A female engineer who scored 99.42 percentile is not selfish, she is competent; the only thing she should fear is the fear itself, not the wanting. Raju closes with what Rancho cannot say from the inside — middle-class fear of choosing your own path is a real, ongoing pain, his own father once said "I was wrong" only after the work was working, parents are also human and can revise themselves later. The rule for the next six years: every month, ask herself — do I still love this? If yes, keep going. If one day no, then pause and rethink. But never let someone else's fear, even her father's, become her own. His generation lived theirs; it is his turn now to watch her live hers.
