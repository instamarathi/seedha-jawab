Summary

Vivek, 23, an engineering graduate from Mangaluru, just landed a respectable bank job — Operations Officer, decent package. His parents threw a party. Relatives came, sweets were distributed, the word "settled" was used freely. Six months in, he can't say anything bad about the job. Colleagues are kind. Manager is fair. Salary is on time. But every morning at 6am he climbs to the terrace and attends an online pottery class — coil method, no wheel, hidden from his parents. He has told no one. He wants to know if he is ungrateful. Rancho points out the obvious: people who are actually ungrateful never ask if they are. Hiding your joy isn't gratitude — it is self-erasure. Don't quit the bank job. Don't make a speech. Tomorrow morning, climb down with one small clay diya and place it in the drawing room. Next month, buy a ₹5,000 wheel. End the terrace. End the hiding. Raju, who once jumped from a third floor over exactly this fear, closes with the truth parents rarely get to say themselves: they don't break when their children chase something small. They break when they discover their child was hiding from them.
