Anand + Bhaskar

Anand has been given six months to live for longer than he can remember. He has no plans to waste them. Bhaskar started out clinical, a keeper of medical notes. He's learning. They'll answer your questions about time — and what to do with what's left of it.

  • My grandfather died last month. Everyone says he is "in a better place." I am 15 and I want a serious answer.
    Thomas, Kottayam
  • My husband died two years ago after fifty years of marriage. People keep telling me he is in a better place. He was in a perfectly fine place — right here.
    Kamala, Varanasi
  • I am 74. I am not afraid of dying anymore. I am afraid of dying before I finish the history of my neighbourhood I have been writing for eleven years.
    Firoz, Kolkata
  • My wife has been bedridden for two years. Sometimes, for a moment, I wish it was over. Is it wrong to wish that, when you love someone that much?
    Jagdish, Barmer
  • My grandmother died while I was filing my PR papers. I have been promoted, bought a condo, called my mother every Sunday. Can I be successful and still be in a grief with no name?
    Sumir, Toronto
  • I am an oncologist. I have spent my career delivering bad news. Last month I received some myself. I have not cried. I am a little afraid of what happens when I do.
    Dr. Venkataraman, Hyderabad
  • Amritsar, her favourite film, the saree she never bought — is this grief, or love in a hurry?
    Ratna, Dharamsala
  • I managed everything when my father died three years ago. I have not cried. Last Saturday I found his reading glasses and sat on the floor for ninety minutes.
    Usha, Bhubaneswar
