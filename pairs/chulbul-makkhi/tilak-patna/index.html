Summary

Tilak Narayan Singh, 72, retired in November 2018 after 38 years as a Senior Clerk in Eastern Central Railway. He lives alone in Kankarbagh, Patna. His wife Saroj died of cancer in October 2021. For four years his pension has been paused — the system says his wife is alive, because the joint life certificate that should have been filed in 2022 was, of course, impossible to file. He has submitted her death certificate seven separate times: in person, by Speed Post, by registered submission, after an RTI, again, again, again. Every time, the file is treated as fresh and prior submissions vanish. He is a former railway clerk himself; he knows this is deliberate, but he cannot prove it. His son in Bangalore sends money. He does not want to be dependent on his son for what is already his — 38 years of service. He writes that he is not brave, just tired. Chulbul Pandey reads it and recognises the loop instantly: Tilak keeps walking back to the same window because that window has the reset button. The fix is not to argue at the window — it is to stop using it. Open four parallel doors at once, none of which the local clerk can touch. One: file a CPGRAMS complaint at pgportal.gov.in — this lives on Cabinet Secretariat servers, generates a tracking ID the local file cannot reset, and forces a 30-day formal reply. Two: meet the DRM Patna in person on a public hearing day, with all seven receipts, the death certificate, and a one-page representation requesting transfer of the file to a new desk. Three: a single email to the local Patna MP with the Minister of Railways' office, the Railway Pension Welfare Board, and the Department of Pension & Pensioners' Welfare in CC — four parallel references that the local desk cannot consolidate or "freshen." Four: in every application, a single paragraph claiming arrears with interest under CCS Pension Rules 2021 and Railway Services (Pension) Rules 1993, because government interest on its own delay must be claimed in writing or it does not get computed. After this, no more visits to the local Pension Section. If they call, be polite — "sir, dhanyavaad, aap dhyan rakh rahe hain" — and let them stay confused. Confused clerks pass files upward, which is exactly the goal. Makkhi adds the human piece: pull the Bangalore son in for the technical work — typing, scanning, CPGRAMS upload — over AnyDesk; this is teamwork, not dependence. Keep the death certificate as a photo on the phone for instant access. Tilak isn't tiring out; he is getting smarter. Realistic timeline: one week to file all four, 30 days for CPGRAMS reply, 45 days for an MP or DRM intervention letter, 60-90 days for file restart, and inside four months the first pension credit with arrears-and-interest order. The local clerk cannot reset the file anymore — only Cabinet Secretariat can, and Cabinet Secretariat doesn't.
