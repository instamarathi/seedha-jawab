नमस्कार sir. मेरा naam Deepak Kushwaha है, उम्र 39 साल, Kanpur में रहता हूँ — Naveen Market के पीछे एक basti में. Auto driver हूँ — पिछले 14 साल से. अपनी auto है, EMI चल रही है, ₹4,200 की हर महीना — अभी 18 EMI और बाकी हैं. Naveen Market वाले corner पे हमारा auto stand है — मैं और 22 दूसरे drivers — यह stand यहाँ 30 साल से ज़्यादा से है, मेरे ससुर भी यहीं चलाते थे, उनके पहले एक चाचा. यह रोज़ी का जगह है, यह हमारी पहचान है.

तीन महीने पहले एक notice आया — Municipal Corporation का — कि stand "relocate" हो रहा है, "beautification cum education hub" बनेगा. हमने पूछा कहाँ shift, बोले "बाद में बताया जाएगा." पर असली बात पता चली — उसी exact corner पे एक coaching centre खुल रहा है, JEE की, और उसके मालिक एक former MLA — पुराने MLA — के बेटे हैं. नाम सब जानते हैं, मैं यहाँ नहीं लिख रहा अभी. यह stand क्यों जा रहा है, समझ में आ रहा है.

उसके बाद RTO ने मुझे बुलाया — "Deepak, अपनी गाड़ी के सब papers ले आओ verify करने के लिए — RC, permit, fitness, insurance, license." मैं ले गया — सब original. RTO साहब ने बोला — "यहाँ रख दो, Thursday को आना." Thursday गया — "अरे आज नहीं हो पाया, Monday आना." Monday — "एक approval आना है RTO के अंदर से, बुधवार आइए." बुधवार — "अभी file ऊपर है, अगले Thursday." पाँच हफ़्ते हो गए हैं sir. Papers RTO के drawer में हैं.

उधर Municipal वाले बोले — "auto stand का relocation NOC RTO से आना चाहिए, तभी हम आपको नया stand allot करेंगे." RTO बोल रहे "वो NOC drafting में है." मतलब दोनों ने मिल के papers मेरे और 22 दूसरों के अपने drawers में रख लिए हैं — एक दूसरे का नाम लेके. मैं बिना papers चला नहीं सकता — पकड़ा गया तो ₹5,000 का चालान, गाड़ी seize. नहीं चला तो EMI नहीं, घर का किराया नहीं, बेटे की school की fees नहीं. पत्नी है, दो बच्चे हैं — बेटा 11, बेटी 8.

Sir, मैं ग़ुस्से में हूँ पर मूर्ख नहीं हूँ. मुझे game पता है — यह सीधा "पकाओ बंदे को, थक जाएगा, उठ के चला जाएगा" वाला game है. पर मैं यहाँ से कहाँ जाऊँ? कोई और काम नहीं आता, 14 साल यही किया है. 22 लोगों का परिवार इस stand से जुड़ा है. मैं पूछना चाहता हूँ — क्या इस सब में कोई असली रास्ता है, या बस एक लंबी line है जिसमें हम ख़ुद को थका कर हार जाएँगे? मुझे बताइए — मुझे वो रास्ता चाहिए जो काम करे, drama नहीं.

— Deepak, Kanpur

Summary

Deepak Kushwaha, 39, has been an auto driver in Kanpur for fourteen years, with a vehicle of his own and an EMI of ₹4,200 a month. The auto stand at Naveen Market — twenty-two drivers, thirty years and more — is being cleared for a "beautification cum education hub" that turns out to be a JEE coaching centre owned by a former MLA's son on the exact same corner. Three months ago, the RTO officer asked him to bring his vehicle papers — RC, permit, fitness, insurance, license — to "verify." All originals. The papers have been in the RTO's drawer for five weeks. Thursday became Monday became next Wednesday. The Municipal officer says the stand can only be relocated when an NOC arrives from the RTO; the RTO says the NOC is "in drafting." Together they are sitting on the papers of all twenty-two drivers, citing each other. Without papers Deepak cannot drive; without driving he cannot pay the EMI, the rent, the school fees of his eleven-year-old son and eight-year-old daughter. He is angry but not stupid; he knows the game is "tire him out, he'll walk away." Chulbul Pandey reads it and gets fired up: this is not bureaucracy, it is theft of livelihood with a stamp paper, two officers playing a classic ping-pong with a man's documents between them. The fix is not to shout at the window — the fix is to escalate, in writing, in five places at once, in a single week, so that neither the RTO nor the Municipal can sit any longer. First, activate the Kanpur Rickshaw Sangh — twenty-two thumb-prints make a complaint that one cannot. Second, a written application at the RTO under Motor Vehicles Act 1988 safe-custody provisions, demanding return of documents in 48 hours, with the District Transport Officer copied, inward stamp taken, and a phone camera ready if the receipt is refused. Third, an RTI on the same day asking under what authority and by whom the documents are retained — the thirty-day clock alone shakes papers loose because reply demands the file be pulled out of the drawer. Fourth, the District Magistrate's Tehsil Diwas / Janta Darshan with the union and a one-page typed complaint that names the MLA's son's coaching centre directly — accountability needs the noun, and the political pressure that is causing the problem can be turned against itself only when the name is on paper. Fifth, a tip to Dainik Bhaskar Kanpur or Amar Ujala, plus a polite Twitter thread tagging the district magistrate, the CM's office, and the UP Transport Department. No more arguments at the RTO window: politeness on the phone, never showing up, letting the officer stay confused while four other doors open. Makkhi adds the practical layer: scan every old photocopy tonight into phone, Gmail draft, and a printed copy at home; never hand an original to a government officer again, ever; keep three copies of every application and receipt in three places; explain the plan to the wife and children so the fear at home is shared, not absorbed alone. Twenty-two families standing together is a small army, and an army does not tire — it changes shifts. Realistic timeline: union meeting tonight, RTO application and RTI tomorrow, DM Janta Darshan within the week, press by Sunday — and in seven to ten days, in most such cases, the papers come out of the drawer, because there is no legal basis to retain them in the first place. The bluff is being called.
