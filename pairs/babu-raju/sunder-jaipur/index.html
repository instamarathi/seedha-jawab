Summary

Sunder Meena, 41, is an auto driver in a Sanganer colony in Jaipur — owns his Mahindra Treo electric on a loan with thirty-one EMIs left. The neighbourhood chit fund — forty families, ₹2,000 a month each, four years running, organised by an "elder" everyone trusted — collapsed last Tuesday when the organiser packed his family into a truck at 11pm and disappeared. ₹32 lakh total gone. Sunder's share: ₹80,000 — five months of dawn-to-night auto driving. ₹30,000 of it was earmarked for his mother's June knee surgery. His wife Geeta has started doing double shifts at a beauty parlour. He has told her "kuch paisa doob gaya hai" but not the number. Auto EMI of ₹6,200 is due in seven days; account balance is ₹4,800. He cannot sleep. Babu Bhaiya recognises this exact panic — he has lived it himself, including the wife-not-told version, and he names the central truth: a hidden number is heavier than the loss itself. The advice arrives in two parts. First, the human one: tell Geeta the number tonight. ₹80,000. She already knows something big has happened — double shifts at a parlour are not for "thoda tight." She is waiting for Sunder to speak. She will cry. He may cry too. The weight that lifts after the number is spoken is real and measurable. Then, the practical: walk into the Mahindra Finance branch tomorrow as a customer, not a defaulter, and request a 3-month moratorium with tenure extension — banks usually approve before a miss, never after. Convene all forty families in the colony hall and file one joint complaint at the SP office (not the SHO) with forty signatures and affidavits — single complaints get buried, forty-family complaints force formal investigation under the Chit Funds Act 1982. File a parallel NCDRC group case online for ₹100 to open a civil track that can attach the organiser's house, vehicle, and relatives' assets — full recovery is unlikely, 30-40% is realistic if the forty stay together. For the surgery, replace the private ₹30,000 plan with SMS Hospital Jaipur under Ayushman Bharat or the CMCHIS Rajasthan (Chiranjeevi Yojana) card — card in hand inside thirty days, free knee replacement. Raju closes with the only thing that needs saying about self-blame: trusting a community fund is not stupidity, it is faith, and from next month the ₹2,000 should still go somewhere — Post Office PPF, or SSY in the daughter's name — lower interest than the committee, but the sleep is better. Tonight: tell Geeta. Tomorrow: forty signatures and one bank visit. The next month is one step a day. It is doable.
