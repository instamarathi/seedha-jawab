Summary

Rohit Kapoor, 29, is a mid-level engineer at the India office of a US product company in Gurgaon, taking home around ₹1.42 lakh a month. He writes in cheerfully ("Hi guys!", with emojis) to share what he calls a slightly funny situation. The list he provides is not funny. Four personal loans across HDFC, Bajaj Finserv, ICICI and IDFC First totalling roughly ₹12.4 lakh of outstanding principal. Two credit cards — HDFC Regalia and SBI Elite — both maxed at full limit. One KreditBee app loan of ₹45,000 he forgot he had taken last Diwali. Total monthly outflow on EMIs and minimum card payments comes to ₹81,400; rent is ₹38,000; living expenses ₹28,000. He needs ₹1.47 lakh a month to stand still. He earns ₹1.42 lakh. He has been ₹6,000 short every single month for nearly a year, and has been covering the gap with a fresh app loan each month — KreditBee, MoneyTap, BharatPe, CASHe in rotation — adding roughly ₹1.5 to ₹2 lakh of high-interest debt purely from patching the shortfall. His parents do not know. His girlfriend has only a vague idea. He is convinced it is "definitely manageable." Babu Bhaiya recognises the smile as the panic mask he himself once wore in 1996, and Raju does the math. The diagnosis is that this is not a budget problem — it is denial wrapped around a cash flow leak that no fresh loan can plug. The plan is sequential. Stop. No new loan, no app, no BNPL, starting tonight; uninstall every loan app on the phone. Build one Google Sheet listing every lender, principal, rate, EMI, tenure and last payment date, and look at the total once. Cut the rent — move from a ₹38,000 1BHK to a ₹15,000 shared 2BHK, unlocking ₹23,000 a month. Cut delivery apps and subscriptions, switch to office cafeteria plus a tiffin service, unlocking another ₹12,000-15,000 a month. Total surplus: roughly ₹35,000 a month. Convert both maxed credit cards to 18-month EMIs at 14-16% via a single phone call to each issuer, saving ₹1.2-1.5 lakh of interest. Then deploy the surplus on the avalanche method — highest interest debt first: cards, then app loans, then personal loans. The KreditBee loan he forgot is the most urgent, because app loans escalate to recovery quickly; check status today and call the lender about a settlement. Across roughly thirty-six months at this discipline, the ₹14-15 lakh debt clears. The non-financial step is the hardest and the most necessary: tell one trusted person — girlfriend, a financially sane college friend, or eventually parents — the actual number, ₹14 lakh, out loud. Three-year debt grinds do not survive in isolation. The smiley face in the letter is a wall between Rohit and his own recovery; it has to come down before any plumbing starts.
