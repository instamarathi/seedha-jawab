सवाल

Khublei. My name is Dilnaz, I am 19, from Shillong — Mawlai area, on the way to Umiam. I am writing this in English-Hindi mix because Khasi is my home language but I don't know if you read Khasi, and the most important parts of this letter are not in any language anyway. कुछ बातें बस होती हैं, कोई भाषा नहीं होती उनकी.

I am a first-generation college student. Mei did not finish school. Pa studied till Class 7. I am in second year B.Com at St. Edmund's, Shillong — हमारे यहाँ commerce respectable degree मानी जाती है, "future secure हो जाएगा" वाली. मेरी fees पाँच साल पहले Pa ने तय की थी कि भरेंगे — कैसे, यह सवाल नहीं था. पिछले साल March में मुझे पता चला कि उन्होंने हमारी पैतृक paddy field, the one near Umroi — which has been with our family for four generations — बेच दी. आधा बिगहा. ₹3.8 lakh में. मुझे यह तब पता चला जब Mei ने एक दिन रोते हुए mistake से मुझे बता दिया. Pa ने कभी ज़िक्र नहीं किया — आज भी नहीं करते. वो बस shop में बैठे रहते हैं — Police Bazaar में एक छोटी सी stationery shop है — और मुझसे college के बारे में पूछते हैं, marks के बारे में, और कभी zameen के बारे में कुछ नहीं.

अब असली confession — मैं रात को कहानियाँ लिखती हूँ. Phone पे, Google Docs में, Khasi में. ग्यारह months लगे एक कहानी पूरी करने में — मेरी Iaid (grandmother) की बात थी, उनके handloom की, उनके finger movements की, उनके पास बैठ के मैंने जो सीखा था weaving के बारे में बचपन में. कहानी का title था "Ka Ryndia" — मतलब वो traditional Khasi shawl जो वो बनाती थीं. मैंने पूरी कर के एक रोज़ रात को 1 बजे read किया — और delete कर दी. पूरी. Backup भी नहीं. मुझे लगा — Papa ने ज़मीन बेची है मेरे लिए commerce पढ़ने को, और मैं रात को Khasi में कहानियाँ लिख रही हूँ? यह तो धोखा है उनके साथ. यह उनके sacrifice का insult है.

तीन महीने हो गए delete किए हुए. मुझे वो story आधी-आधी हर रात याद आती है. कुछ lines exact याद हैं — Iaid की उंगलियों के बारे में, eri silk के बारे में, उस आख़िरी बार के बारे में जब मैं उनके पास बैठी थी. मैं फिर से लिखने बैठती हूँ — फिर रुक जाती हूँ. Commerce notes खोलती हूँ. Tally seekhati हूँ. सो जाती हूँ.

मेरा सवाल यह है — क्या मैं वाक़ई selfish हूँ? Pa ने ज़मीन बेच के commerce के लिए पैसे दिए — और मैं रात को कुछ और चाह रही हूँ. क्या यह betrayal है? क्या यह gratitude की कमी है? Please सच बताइए. कोई और मुझे यह सवाल पूछने को नहीं है — Mei को बोल नहीं सकती (वो रोएँगी), Pa को तो नामुमकिन है, friends को बोलूँगी तो वो "तू तो genius है, लिखती रह" वाला flat answer देंगी जो मेरी मदद नहीं करता. मुझे कोई ऐसा चाहिए जो दोनों तरफ़ देख सके.

— Dilnaz, Shillong

Pa ने paddy field बेची नहीं — Pa ने तुझे एक gift दी, "तू जो भी चाहे वो बन." तू उन्हें यह gift वापस मत कर के बोल "मैं छोटी रहूँगी ताकि आपने जो किया वो ठीक लगे." Pa को छोटी बेटी नहीं चाहिए. Pa को असली बेटी चाहिए. वो लिख रही है — रात को. वो रहने दे, हमेशा.

Summary

Dilnaz, 19, is a first-generation college student in Shillong studying B.Com at St. Edmund's. Her mother did not finish school; her father studied till Class 7. Last year she found out by accident — her mother let it slip while crying — that her father had quietly sold a four-generation-old paddy field near Umroi for 3.8 lakh rupees to pay her college fees. Her father has never mentioned the sale and never will. Dilnaz writes short stories in Khasi on her phone at night. After eleven months she finished one — "Ka Ryndia," about her grandmother Iaid's traditional Khasi handloom and the eri silk shawls she wove. She read it once at 1 a.m., decided she was betraying her father by wanting more than commerce, and deleted it. No backup. Three months later she still remembers the exact lines. Rancho refuses her framing entirely. Her father did not sell paddy fields for commerce — he sold them so his daughter could become bigger than he was able to. Commerce was the language available to him, not the limit of his intention. Stopping at commerce-graduate fulfills only the literal half of what he wanted; the real half is for Dilnaz to become her full self — commerce and writer, an "and," not an "or." The deletion was wrong: Iaid's loom was a civilizational record only Dilnaz could write, not a self-indulgence. Farhan, who once deleted a five-month dhobi-ghat photo series after one cutting comment from his CA father and has dreamt of those frames for seven years, recognizes the exact pattern and steps in personally. The plan is precise. First, tonight, restart the story under the same title — one page, the lines are still inside her. Second, back up in three places — phone, Google Drive, weekly self-email — because writers protect their files. Third, a quiet research project: ask the mother stories about Iaid's weaving over chai, framed as nothing more than curiosity, which both gives her details and gives the mother a small joy. Fourth, do not tell the father yet — he needs an undisturbed certainty for now. In about two years, when one story appears in a Khasi periodical like Mawphor or U Nongsain Hima, hand him the print. His generation legitimises through paper. Until then, write at night, in Khasi first, English translation only later, the original always preserved. Farhan closes with the line Rancho once said to him after his own deletion: she has not failed to keep her father's heart — she would, in fact, fail him by becoming smaller than herself. The father did not give a fee; he gave a gift of choice. Don't return that gift wrapped in self-erasure.
